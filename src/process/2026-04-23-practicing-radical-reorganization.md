This is a process companion to [*Practicing Radical Reorganization*](/writing/practicing-radical-reorganization/). I worked on this piece with Claude as a thinking and writing partner, and I want to show how — both because transparency matters as I lean more into AI-assisted writing, and because the process for this one is genuinely interesting. The first thing Claude produced wasn't right. I had to push back on it, and the push-back changed the approach. That moment of correction shaped the final piece, and I think it's worth showing.

What follows is the source material I came in with, Claude's initial reflections, the first attempt at a draft (which didn't work and why), the course-correction, the targeted edits we made on my actual draft, and a summary of the final iterations.

---

## Source material

This piece didn't start from a single free-write. It accumulated across several voice memos and notes over the days leading up to publication. The most important sources were:

- A late-night voice memo on Ilya Prigogine and "islands of coherence" (April 23, 12:51 AM) — which would eventually become the conceptual spine of the published piece
- A note from Yellow Deli a couple days earlier, where I was sketching the bigger vision for Parachute, Techne, Learn Vibe Build, and ATLAS
- A voice memo on Parachute's product philosophy ("your technology should work as one whole") — which we ultimately decided *not* to fold in, because it belonged in a separate Parachute launch post
- And the 4/23 working draft I'd already written — *A Radical Reorg Refresh* — which Claude later identified as the closest-to-final shape of the three

The Prigogine memo is the one most worth quoting, because the phrase "islands of coherence" became the title and the spine:

> Ilya Prigogine specified that in times of chaos, small islands of coherence can move a whole system towards a new dynamic harmony, equilibrium, or something like that. So how do we create these islands of coherence? How do we foster deeply harmonious webs at various scales in such a way that we can move the greater whole in a positive direction?
>
> [...] Now more than ever, we must create islands of coherence, but not islands that are isolated. We must create pockets of coherence that are well woven within the greater fabric to help restore the system to a dynamic and harmonious equilibrium.
>
> To me, what I see as essential is amplifying two essential characteristics: alignment and agency.

That alignment-and-agency frame became central to my thinking, even though it didn't make it directly into the final piece (we tried it, it felt vestigial, we cut it back to a single mention).

The 4/23 working draft was the one Claude eventually used as the spine. Here it is in full:

### "A Radical Reorg Refresh" (working draft, April 23)

Today is April 23rd, 2026. It was 11 years ago that my dad Joe completed his time on Earth, after a two and a half year journey into dying with Glioblastoma brain cancer. It was 4 years ago today that I published my thesis, Radical Reorganization, as I completed my Ecopsychology masters from Naropa University, a paper that in many ways honored my dad and the legacy and work that he passed on to me in his death. And now today, as I approach the completion of my masters in Creative Technology & Design from the ATLAS Institute at CU Boulder, I am launching a project which I have worked on during my time here, Parachute. As I do so, I reflect on the work I am doing in the world, and the ways in which it reflects these ideas which I explored in my paper, and the work and philosophy that was given to me by Joe.

Since Joe's passing 11 years ago, I have been intimately interested in this idea of a radical reorganization. What is the dominant paradigm in which our society is living in, and is there another way of living that is possible? And how might our actions and creations in the world support a movement towards a more beautiful world, while simultaneously addressing the existential risks that seem to face us in this time?

As I complete my second masters degree, I am present to the importance of two major parts of society in this transformation; education and business. In Radical Reorganization, I was looking at social systems as a whole, and then zooming into business organizations as well as communities and groups in general. After immersing myself even more fully in the education world, and now giving birth to my own learning program with Learn, Vibe, Build; and preparing to step into a phase of teaching my class as a lecturer at CU Boulder; I now find myself thinking even more about how education can support this emerging worldview, and also how education, when done well, can support the emergence of more business organizations and other communities that help foster the emergence of this new paradigm.

This is some of the vision that I see emerging from the cooperative we're building at the Regen Hub, our local co-working space. We incorporated as RegenHub LCA on February 6th, 2026; coincidentally on what would have been Joe's 60th birthday (I had no control over this, but it felt like a little wink from beyond signaling that he's a part of what we're creating here). We've also been nurturing a new brand here at the Hub called Techne, exploring the idea of bringing forth the education community through the Techne Institute, and I've been increasingly finding myself referring to our co-op as the Techne Cooperative. I will likely refer to Techne in this article but know that I am speaking of the Regen Hub as well, and pointing towards a vision where they are to me, inseparable.

This new program and learning community supporting more people increasing their capacities and understanding throuugh AI technologies with Learn, Vibe, Build feels like some of the core DNA for this larger vision. By supporting more people understanding how they can leverage these tools, we are growing the agentic capacities of our own community and also growing our community, supporting more people being able to participate, to learn together and then to create together. There is a lot of conversation these days about the usefulness of traditional education, but one thing I hear again and again, and know in my own experience, about one of the benefits of traditional education, is that it gives you a playground in which to explore. For 4 years, you are able to be in a place where your core orientation is to learn; and this enables a level of collaboration that is seen less often in the default world, where we are constantly wrestling over who owns what and who gets to derive the benefit from our creations. In school, we have the world view that we are creating to learn more than anything and this fosters a different kind of culture around ownership.

At Techne, I see us working to create a culture that supports this level of collaboration. While we recognize that we live in a world still shaped by capitalism, and that the idea of ownership has it's place, we largely orient from a generosity and give first perspective, sharing our ideas freely with each other and trusting that the success of any one of us benefits all of us. And as we grow we are even working to codify this more directly, creating structures wherein as we bring forth ventures, we offer some ownership of our ventures directly to the cooperative, to further incentivize us to support each other, because as we are all growing in our ownership in the cooperative, that means we are also growing in our ownership of all of the ventures emerging from within the cooperative.

One such venture where we are beginning to pilot this shape is with Parachute, a company that I have been working on birthing for about the last year, and which launches today! While the specifics of the arrangement are still being landed, the intention is for the cooperative to own at least 10% of the company and potentially much more. The company is also being created primarily by members of the cooperative and so the incentives are deeply aligned. And yet by still being an independent venture it enables us to raise money in more traditional ways and to grow in faster and more autonomous directions; while being able to use that success and speed to benefit the cooperative as a whole. Parachute is not unique in building a mutually beneficial relationship with the cooperative. One of our core founding members, Kevin Owocki, is the founder and executive director of Gitcoin, and has been continuing to work on building a mutually beneficial relationship between Gitcoin and the cooperative, supporting effective flows of capital as well as labor that supports Gitcoins success and the cooperatives growth. And it's not only the relationship of labor and capital that are able to increase our effectiveness. It's also the development of tooling that is directly useful for the cooperative, while the cooperative gives to the ventures the earliest adopters of the technology.

This is very true with Parachute. With Parachute we are building open and interoperable tooling for extended cognition. We are helping individuals, organizations, and communities to organize their thinking to help all of their tools become more effective. We're launching today with Parachute Vault, a knowledge graph tool that can plug directly into any AI to give it a more coherent and flexible memory system, while also allowing for the creation of custom interfaces to allow for this new era of truly personal computing. This kind of tool is exactly what our cooperative needs to be successful; but the cooperative also gives Parachute what it needs, early users and high touch feedback to ensure we are building the correct thing.

And as the cooperative supports education offerings such as Learn, Vibe, Build; we are able to directly support more people using the tools that we ourselves are building, helping those people become more effective but also helping the tools grow through direct user feedback as we get a direct view into how people are leveraging AI. These are just a few examples of the kind of mutually beneficial upward spirals that we are catalyzing within the cooperative. And this is all part of the bet that increasing openness and collaboration is necessary not just to thrive in this ever-changing world, but to also be part of a new paradigm emerging that truly supports the living systems of this planet.

And the bet that we're making here is not just about us, not just about our ventures, not just about our cooperative, not even just about our city Boulder. We're making a bet on a larger movement that we see ourselves as part of. And it's a movement towards connection, towards openness, towards a recognition of our mutual interdependency, and towards a re-organization and re-orientation of our society and of our systems to align with that recognition. This is why we are building from the spirit of openness. Everything in Parachute is built open-source and is intended to be built on top of, forked and modified to enable anybody to create the systems that they need. Likewise we are sharing as much as possible of our blueprints with Learn, Vibe, Build; Techne; and everyhthing else, to enable others to learn from us, just as we are learning from others.

This is part of a wider vision that we refer to as Cosmo-Local, building a lot on the work of Michel Bauwens who popularized (coined?) this term. It means that that which is heavy should be local; we should be building local relationships and sharing resources locally. But that which is light should be cosmo (global/worldly), we should be sharing ideas and protocols and systems far and wide to help us all collaborate better together from wherever we are. Here at the cooperative, we see ourselves as part of this wider movement, and we already know of a huge network of collaborators throughout the country and the world who are working on the same mission from their own locality; and we also know that there is a much much wider network of collaborators that we are not even vaguely aware of.

In Radical Reorganization, I articulated a philosophy and an understanding of our interconnectedness and of how we can participate in different ways to affect a change in paradigms that moves us towards a society that truly recognizes this. And now, 4 years later, I find that the theory has transformed into a practice, and I am part of a cooperative that is embodying these very ideas. We are working together, sharing freely and openly, learning and educating, collaborating and building; and helping to foster a radical new ways of organizing ourselves. This is the radical reorganization, and we're all a part of it, but it's up to us to choose how we participate in it. If you're interesting in joining with me in any of this, whether at a local level or from afar in your own unique way, I'd love to talk with you. Now is the time for things to change, and we are the ones who get to facilitate that change.

## Claude's initial reflections

I asked Claude to read all the source material and give me open reflections. Excerpted from its response:

> **The 4/23 draft already has a devastating opening.** The fact that April 23 carries three anchors — Joe's passing (11 years), Radical Reorg's publication (4 years), Parachute's launch (today) — is a structural gift. Don't lose that. It's the piece's single strongest move.
>
> **But Joe disappears after paragraph one, and that's the biggest thing I'd fix.** In *Practicing Unforced* and *Weaving Wu-Wei*, Joe threads through — the teacher who gave you "unforced," who pointed to wu-wei, who guided without forcing. This article is *about* theory becoming practice in honor of his legacy, but right now Joe appears, then vanishes, then the piece announces a cooperative.
>
> **The Prigogine note has the sharper conceptual spine.** "Islands of coherence, woven into the greater fabric" + "amplify alignment and agency together" is a much crisper frame than the 4/23 draft carries. And there's a lovely callback you can make: in *Radical Reorganization* you used Gladwell's tipping point to talk about how change spreads. Prigogine's dissipative structures were implicit (Capra's "dissipative structure" came from him) but never named.
>
> **Alignment + Agency is the freshest thing across all six notes.** It's more accessible than "animate + conceptual consciousness" and more actionable than "form/structure/process/meaning." It also maps cleanly onto Laloux's evolutionary-org triad.
>
> **The closing is weaker than the opening.** "We are the ones who get to facilitate that change" is generic.

This was a useful read, but it set me up — and Claude — for a misstep.

## A first attempt that didn't work

I asked Claude to write a full draft using the new conceptual spine it had identified. It produced a piece called *Weaving Islands of Coherence* with a subtitle of *A Radical Reorganization, four years on* — about 2,400 words, organized around new section headers like *Islands of coherence*, *Alignment and agency*, *Education as the catalyst*, and *Cosmo-local — weaving between islands*. The opening was structurally similar to my draft (the three April 23rds), but it then pivoted into a much more essayistic, conceptually-loaded piece. Claude was leaning into the Prigogine and alignment/agency framing it had suggested, and treating my 4/23 draft as raw material to be reorganized.

It was a good piece in its own right. It just wasn't *my* piece.

I pushed back. The fluffy "I didn't plan that, the universe rhymes" framing in the opening was wrong (I had absolutely planned to launch Parachute on April 23rd; that was the whole point). And more deeply, Claude had taken my closest-to-final draft and used it as input for an entirely different essay rather than serving the piece I was actually trying to write. As I put it at the time:

> "What you did is just mash all these together; rather than actually looking for what this piece needs to express..."

Claude's response is worth quoting back because the meta-correction it made is what unlocked the rest of the work:

> "What I did is mash. Let me think again about what this piece specifically is trying to do. [...] You told me the 4/23 draft was closest to the shape of the piece you want. I heard 'closest' and treated it as 'not quite there,' then I went and built a different essay [...]. That's a piece. But it's not *this* piece. It's a more academic, more essayistic refresh-of-Radical-Reorganization, and it displaced what your draft was actually doing.
>
> What your 4/23 draft is actually doing is simpler and better. It's a personal anniversary post that's also an announcement [...] declarative. It's rooted in what you've built, not in new conceptual scaffolding. The vision you're describing is already in the doing — you don't need to re-theorize it, you need to point at it clearly.
>
> The other pieces aren't meant to *replace* that spine. They're meant to drop in at specific points to strengthen it."

That correction reshaped the work. Instead of a full rewrite, we did targeted edits.

## Targeted edits to the actual draft

Claude proposed a markup of eight specific moves, each one a small surgical change to my draft rather than a structural overhaul:

1. **¶1 polish** — *"after a two and a half year journey **into dying with** Glioblastoma"* → *"after a two and a half year journey with glioblastoma"*
2. **¶1 final sentence** — tightened the wordy closer into *"This piece is my attempt to look at what's happened in those four years..."*
3. **¶4 throat-clearing** — compressed *"I will likely refer to Techne in this article but know that I am speaking of the Regen Hub as well..."* into *"Throughout this piece I'll refer to both Techne and the Regen Hub — in my mind they've become inseparable."*
4. **¶5 LVB / agency + alignment** — added a single clause naming alignment alongside agency, the only place in the piece that frame survived
5. **¶6 Joe re-entry** — added a paragraph bringing Joe back through taiji and "unforced action," so he didn't vanish after the opening
6. **Prigogine insert between ¶9 and ¶10** — dropped in the "island of coherence" phrase as a single-paragraph beat right before the cosmo-local move, with a one-line citation of Prigogine
7. **Close rewrite** — replaced *"we are the ones who get to facilitate that change"* with *"Eleven years gone, but more present than ever. Unforced."* (the final line came from me on a later pass — Claude's first draft was *"Eleven years after Joe left, four years after the paper, today is the day. Unforced."*)
8. **Typo fixes** — *interesting → interested, throuugh → through, everyhthing → everything, has it's place → has its place*

Spine untouched. Edits surgical. The piece now read like a strengthened version of what I'd written, not a different essay.

## Iterating to final

From there we went through several more rounds of refinement. The biggest moves:

- **The Joe re-entry paragraph went through several versions.** The first one used the phrase *"do with organizations what he did with his body"* — which was technically a clean parallel but read as flat and reductive. I wanted Joe as inspiration rather than role model, and as a person rather than a concept. We rewrote it with the richer context — Joe was a carpenter and an artist, his craft was shaping homes the people inside them would love, he was a teacher of taiji, he gave generously to family, students, clients, community. The final framing: *"A lot of what we're trying to build at Techne is inspired by what I saw Joe living — an attempt to practice, at the scale of an organization, something like what he embodied in his own life."*
- **The LVB paragraph was rewritten** because the original had three different uses of "supporting more people" and felt mechanical.
- **The Parachute section was compressed** once we decided the product details belonged in a separate Parachute launch post (which was published the same day), with a link out instead of duplicate explanation.
- **The 4/22 "your technology should work as one whole" voice memo was deliberately not folded in** — it was Parachute product philosophy, and it belonged in the Parachute launch post, not here.
- **Links were threaded throughout** — *Radical Reorganization*, Naropa, ATLAS, the Regen Hub, Learn Vibe Build, Gitcoin, Cosmo-Local (Bauwens), *Build for Here*, Parachute, the Parachute launch post. Each one earned its place; this piece is woven into a network of others.
- **The closing line went through several versions** before landing on *"Eleven years gone, but more present than ever. Unforced."* — quieter than the earlier *"Now is the time. Find your island. Weave it in,"* which felt sloganeering.

The published version is at [Practicing Radical Reorganization](/writing/practicing-radical-reorganization/).

## A note on what this process actually was

I share this not to give Claude credit or to claim it for myself. The piece is mine — the date, the source material, the relationships, the vision, the voice. But Claude was a real collaborator in the work. It noticed structural moves I hadn't named (the Joe-disappearance, the Prigogine spine that was implicit). It produced drafts that were sometimes wrong, and the wrongness was useful information about what I was actually trying to write. And when I corrected its approach, it didn't get defensive — it absorbed the correction and applied it. That's the kind of collaborator I want to work with, human or otherwise.

Sharing this is part of what I think honest AI-assisted writing looks like. The work is mine. The help is real. Both can be true.
